On the Wing
Mapping Bird Migration

Every year, billions of birds leave their breeding grounds and travel thousands of kilometres to spend the season elsewhere. Using satellite GPS tracking and birdwatcher observations from eBird, this visualisation maps the routes of four species across the globe, and explains why these migrations are worth paying attention to.

Section 02 — eBird Citizen Science
Seasonal Abundance — The Migration Wave

Citizen scientists reported millions of sightings via eBird throughout 2023. Each dot below represents a 27 km grid cell coloured by relative abundance — how many birds were seen there in a given week. Use the slider to watch the migration wave move across the globe. Toggle species on and off to compare patterns.

What to look for: Grey Plovers peak along European coasts in autumn (Sep–Oct) before disappearing to West Africa. The Barn Swallow floods Europe in spring (Apr–May) and retreats south by August. The Upland Sandpiper concentrates tightly in the Great Plains before its trans-equatorial sprint to South America.

Section 04 — Movement Analysis
How Birds Actually Move

GPS data lets us see not just where birds go, but how they move. The latitude chart shows the north-south journey of each species across the calendar year — the clearest way to see migration happening. The distance charts reveal how far birds travel on their biggest days, versus a typical moving day.
